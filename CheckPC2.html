Real Security Tips:

Don't click on anything in sketchy pop-ups, not even the "X"

Why this matters:

  • Many fake "X" buttons actually trigger malware downloads
  • Use Alt+F4 or Task Manager to close suspicious windows
Never download anything a pop-up tells you to

Safe Download Practices:

  • Only download from official vendor websites
  • Check file extensions (eg: .exe)
  • Scan all downloads with antivirus before opening
Use your browser's built-in pop-up blocker

Browser Protection:

  • Make sure pop-up blocking and safe browsing features are turned on in your browser settings.
  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Site Settings > Pop-ups
Check the website URL carefully

URL Red Flags:

  • Look for HTTPS:// and padlock icon
  • Watch for misspellings
  • Be wary of domains ending in .xyz, .top, .gq
Keep your antivirus and browser up to date

Update Benefits:

  • Security patches fix known vulnerabilities
  • Newer versions detect more malware variants
Never enter personal info on pop-up pages

Data Protection:

  • Legitimate sites won't ask for credentials via pop-up
  • Close the window and navigate directly to the official site
  • Use password managers to avoid phishing sites
